=== CONJECTURER ROUND ===
Let \(O\) be the origin, and write the position vectors of \(A,B,C,G,H\) as \(a,b,c,g,h\in \mathbb R^2\).

Since \(O\) is the circumcenter,
\[
\|a\|=\|b\|=\|c\|=R
\]
for some \(R\), and since \(G\) is the centroid,
\[
g=\frac{a+b+c}{3}.
\]

The three perpendicularity conditions say exactly that \(H\) is the orthocenter of triangle \(AOG\). With \(O=0\), two of them become
\[
(h-a)\cdot g=0,\qquad (h-g)\cdot a=0,
\]
so
\[
h\cdot g=a\cdot g,\qquad h\cdot a=a\cdot g.
\]
Hence
\[
h\cdot (b+c)=h\cdot(3g-a)=3(h\cdot g)-(h\cdot a)=2(a\cdot g).
\]

Now \(H\) lies on line \(BC\). Since \(\|b\|=\|c\|\), the line \(BC\) is perpendicular to \(b+c\), so every point \(x\) on \(BC\) satisfies
\[
x\cdot (b+c)=R^2+b\cdot c.
\]
Thus for \(x=h\),
\[
R^2+b\cdot c=2(a\cdot g)=\frac{2}{3}(R^2+a\cdot b+a\cdot c).
\]

Convert dot products to side lengths:
\[
AB^2=\|a-b\|^2=121 \implies a\cdot b=R^2-\frac{121}{2},
\]
\[
AC^2=\|a-c\|^2=169 \implies a\cdot c=R^2-\frac{169}{2},
\]
and if \(BC=x\), then
\[
b\cdot c=R^2-\frac{x^2}{2}.
\]
Substitute:
\[
R^2+\left(R^2-\frac{x^2}{2}\right)
=\frac{2}{3}\left(R^2+R^2-\frac{121}{2}+R^2-\frac{169}{2}\right).
\]
So
\[
2R^2-\frac{x^2}{2}=2R^2-\frac{290}{3},
\]
hence
\[
\frac{x^2}{2}=\frac{290}{3},\qquad x^2=\frac{580}{3}.
\]
Therefore
\[
BC=\sqrt{\frac{580}{3}}.
\]
