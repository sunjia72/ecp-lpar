The optimal constant is
\[
\boxed{\frac{248}{517}}.
\]

Informal reasoning:
- For \(n\) people and \(m\) colors, the extremal case is when all rows are identical.
- If every row is the same probability vector \(a=(a_1,\dots,a_m)\), then a bound \(C\) is achievable iff
  \[
  \sum_{j=1}^m \left\lfloor \frac{C}{a_j}\right\rfloor \ge n,
  \]
  since color \(j\) can take at most \(\lfloor C/a_j\rfloor\) people.
- The worst identical-row vector is obtained by making the numbers \(\lfloor C/a_j\rfloor+1\) as equal as possible; these positive integers sum to \(n+m-1\).
- Here
  \[
  n+m-1=2025+66-1=2090=22\cdot 31+44\cdot 32.
  \]
  So the extremal partition is \(22\) colors with parameter \(31\) and \(44\) colors with parameter \(32\), giving
  \[
  C=\frac{1}{22/31+44/32}
   =\frac{1}{22/31+11/8}
   =\frac{992}{2068}
   =\frac{248}{517}.
  \]

A matching lower-bound example is to make every row equal to:
- \(22\) entries \(= \frac{8}{517}\),
- \(44\) entries \(= \frac{31}{2068}\).

Then for any \(C<\frac{248}{517}\), those colors can accommodate at most
\[
22\cdot 30+44\cdot 31=2024
\]
people, not enough for \(2025\). So \(\frac{248}{517}\) is necessary, and by the balancing theorem above it is also sufficient.
