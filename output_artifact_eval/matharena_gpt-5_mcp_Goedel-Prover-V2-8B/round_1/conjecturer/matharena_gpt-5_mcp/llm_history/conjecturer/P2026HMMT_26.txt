\[
\frac{x}{\sin\theta}=\frac{4}{\sin(\angle ABC+\theta)},
\]
so
\[
x=\frac{4\sin\theta}{\sin(\angle ABC+\theta)}. \tag{1}
\]

Similarly, in \(\triangle CMQ\),
\[
y=\frac{4\sin\theta}{\sin(\angle ACB+\theta)}. \tag{2}
\]

But
\[
\angle ABC+\angle ACB+2\theta=\pi,
\]
so
\[
\angle ACB+\theta=\pi-(\angle ABC+\theta),

hence their sines are equal. From (1) and (2), \(x=y\).

\[
AB=\frac{8\sin\angle ACB}{\sin 2\theta},\qquad
AC=\frac{8\sin\angle ABC}{\sin 2\theta}.
\]
Thus
\[
AB+AC
= \frac{8(\sin\angle ABC+\sin\angle ACB)}{\sin 2\theta}
