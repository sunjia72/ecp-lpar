\[
q(x)=x^3-x=x(x-1)(x+1),\qquad n=1992,\qquad m=n+1=1993.
\]

For any valid \(p\), divide by \(q\):
\[
p(x)=q(x)s(x)+r(x),\qquad \deg r<3.
\]
Since \(\deg p<1992\), we have \(\deg s\le 1988\), so \(s^{(1992)}=0\). Thus
\[
\left(\frac{p}{q}\right)^{(1992)}=\left(\frac{r}{q}\right)^{(1992)}.
\]

Because \(p\) is coprime to \(q\), also \(r(-1),r(0),r(1)\neq 0\). Hence
\[
\frac{r(x)}{q(x)}=\frac{A}{x}+\frac{B}{x-1}+\frac{C}{x+1}
\]
with \(A,B,C\neq 0\).

Differentiating \(n=1992\) times gives
\[
\left(\frac{r}{q}\right)^{(1992)}
=1992!\left(\frac{A}{x^{m}}+\frac{B}{(x-1)^m}+\frac{C}{(x+1)^m}\right).
\]
So the reduced denominator has poles of order \(m\) at each of \(-1,0,1\), hence denominator degree \(3m\).

Now expand at infinity:
\[
\frac{A}{x^m}+\frac{B}{(x-1)^m}+\frac{C}{(x+1)^m}
=
x^{-m}\left((A+B+C)+m(B-C)x^{-1}+\frac{m(m+1)}2(B+C)x^{-2}+\cdots\right).
\]
So the first nonzero term can be delayed by at most 2 powers:
- \(x^{-m}\) vanishes iff \(A+B+C=0\),
- \(x^{-m-1}\) vanishes iff \(B=C\),
- then \(x^{-m-2}\) has coefficient \(\frac{m(m+1)}2(B+C)=m(m+1)B\neq 0\).

Thus the maximum decay is \(x^{-(m+2)}\), so the numerator degree is at least
\[
3m-(m+2)=2m-2.
\]
This bound is attained by taking \(B=C\) and \(A=-2B\), e.g. \(r(x)\) constant nonzero, so \(p(x)=1\) works.
