Let  
\[
I(f)=\int_0^1 |f'(x)-f(x)|\,dx.
\]

We seek the greatest \(u\) such that \(I(f)\ge u\) for every smooth \(f\) with \(f(0)=0,\ f(1)=1\).

Set
\[
h(x)=f'(x)-f(x).
\]
Then
\[
(e^{-x}f(x))'=e^{-x}(f'(x)-f(x))=e^{-x}h(x).
\]
Integrating from \(0\) to \(1\),
\[
e^{-1}f(1)-f(0)=\int_0^1 e^{-x}h(x)\,dx.
\]
Using \(f(0)=0,\ f(1)=1\), this becomes
\[
e^{-1}=\int_0^1 e^{-x}h(x)\,dx.
\]
Hence
\[
e^{-1}
= \left|\int_0^1 e^{-x}h(x)\,dx\right|
\le \int_0^1 e^{-x}|h(x)|\,dx
\le \int_0^1 |h(x)|\,dx
= I(f).
\]
So every admissible \(f\) satisfies
\[
I(f)\ge e^{-1}.
\]

Now we show this is sharp. Take \(h_n\ge 0\) smooth, supported very near \(0\), chosen so that
\[
\int_0^1 e^{-x}h_n(x)\,dx=e^{-1}.
\]
Then define
\[
f_n(x)=e^x\int_0^x e^{-t}h_n(t)\,dt.
\]
This gives smooth \(f_n\) with \(f_n(0)=0,\ f_n(1)=1\), and
\[
f_n'(x)-f_n(x)=h_n(x).
\]
Therefore
\[
I(f_n)=\int_0^1 h_n(x)\,dx.
\]
Because \(h_n\) is concentrated near \(0\), where \(e^{-x}\approx 1\), the condition
\[
\int_0^1 e^{-x}h_n(x)\,dx=e^{-1}
\]
forces
\[
\int_0^1 h_n(x)\,dx \to e^{-1}.
\]
So the lower bound \(e^{-1}\) can be approached arbitrarily closely.
