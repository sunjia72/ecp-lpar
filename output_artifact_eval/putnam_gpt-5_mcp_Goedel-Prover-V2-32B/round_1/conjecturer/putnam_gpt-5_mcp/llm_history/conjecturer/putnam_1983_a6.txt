### Asymptotic reasoning
The exponent \(x^3+y^3\) on the triangle \(x,y\ge 0,\ x+y\le a\) is maximized at the two corners \((a,0)\) and \((0,a)\), with value \(a^3\). So only small neighborhoods of those corners contribute after multiplying by \(e^{-a^3}\).

By symmetry, twice the contribution near \((a,0)\) suffices. On the half-triangle \(x\ge y\), set
\[
u=a-x,\qquad v=y.
\]
Then near the corner \((a,0)\), we have \(0\le v\le u\), and
\[
x^3+y^3=(a-u)^3+v^3.
\]
The significant scale is \(u,v\sim a^{-2}\), so write
\[
u=\frac{s}{a^2},\qquad v=\frac{t}{a^2},
\qquad 0\le t\le s.
\]
Then
\[
(a-u)^3+v^3-a^3
=
\left(a-\frac{s}{a^2}\right)^3+\left(\frac{t}{a^2}\right)^3-a^3
=
-3s+o(1),
\]
and \(du\,dv=a^{-4}\,ds\,dt\), so the factor \(a^4\) cancels the Jacobian.

Hence one corner contributes
\[
\int_0^\infty\int_0^s e^{-3s}\,dt\,ds
=
\int_0^\infty s e^{-3s}\,ds
=
\frac19.
\]
There are two symmetric corners, so
\[
\lim_{a\to\infty} F(a)=2\cdot \frac19=\frac29.
\]
