- all other coordinates unchanged.

1. \(M(s)\) is adjacent to \(s\), so \(\{s,M(s)\}\) is a legal move-edge.
2. \(M(M(s))=s\).
3. Every non-initial state belongs to exactly one such pair.

So this is a perfect matching on all vertices except the initial state.

- after Alice moves from the initial state to some \(s\), Bob moves to \(M(s)\);
- thereafter, whenever Alice moves to some new state \(t\), Bob moves to its matched partner \(M(t)\).
