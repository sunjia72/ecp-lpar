Let \(M(n)\) be the greatest \(m\) in the statement.

I claim
\[
M(n)=n.
\]

Sketch:

- Write \(a_i=x(2i-1)\), \(b_i=x(2i)\), so
  \[
  -1<a_1<b_1<a_2<b_2<\cdots<a_n<b_n<1.
  \]
  The condition is
  \[
  \sum_{i=1}^n (b_i^{2k-1}-a_i^{2k-1})=1 \qquad (k=1,\dots,m).
  \]

## Upper bound \(m\le n\)

Let \(E=\bigcup_{i=1}^n [a_i,b_i]\subset (-1,1)\).  
Since
\[
b_i^{2k-1}-a_i^{2k-1}=(2k-1)\int_{a_i}^{b_i} t^{2k-2}\,dt,
\]
the hypothesis becomes
\[
\int_E t^{2k-2}\,dt=\int_0^1 t^{2k-2}\,dt \qquad (k=1,\dots,m).
\]
After the change of variables \(u=t^2\), this says
\[
\int_0^1 u^{k-1}\phi(u)\,du=0 \qquad (k=1,\dots,m),
\]
where
\[
\phi(u)=\frac{\mathbf 1_E(\sqrt u)+\mathbf 1_E(-\sqrt u)-1}{\sqrt u}.
\]
So \(\phi\) is orthogonal to every polynomial of degree \(<m\).

Now \(\phi\) has the same sign as
\[
N(u)-1,\qquad N(u)=\mathbf 1_E(\sqrt u)+\mathbf 1_E(-\sqrt u)\in\{0,1,2\}.
\]
As \(u\) increases, \(N(u)\) is a step function changing by \(\pm1\), and because \(E\) is a union of \(n\) intervals, it can cross the level \(1\) at most \(n\) times. Hence \(\phi\) has at most \(n\) sign changes.

But a standard fact says: if a nonzero integrable function is orthogonal to all polynomials of degree \(<m\), then it must have at least \(m\) sign changes. Therefore \(m\le n\).

## Lower bound \(m\ge n\)

Take
\[
\theta=\frac{\pi}{2n+1},\qquad x(j)=-\cos(j\theta)\quad (j=1,\dots,2n),
\]
and extend \(x\) outside \(\{1,\dots,2n\}\) in any strictly increasing way. Then
\[
-1<x(1)<\cdots<x(2n)<1.
\]

For any odd \(q\le 2n-1\),
\[
\sum_{j=1}^{2n}(-1)^{j+1}e^{iqj\theta}=1,
\]
by a geometric-series computation with \(z=-e^{iq\theta}\), since \(z^{2n+1}=1\). Taking real parts,
\[
\sum_{j=1}^{2n}(-1)^{j+1}\cos(qj\theta)=1.
\]

Now for odd \(r\),
\[
\cos^r t
\]
is a linear combination of \(\cos(qt)\) with odd \(q\le r\), and the coefficients sum to \(1\). Hence for every odd \(r\le 2n-1\),
\[
\sum_{j=1}^{2n}(-1)^{j+1}\cos^r(j\theta)=1.
\]
Since \(x(j)=-\cos(j\theta)\) and \(r\) is odd,
\[
\sum_{i=1}^n\bigl(x(2i)^r-x(2i-1)^r\bigr)=1.
\]
Putting \(r=2k-1\), this holds for \(k=1,\dots,n\). So \(m\ge n\).

Combining both bounds gives \(M(n)=n\).
