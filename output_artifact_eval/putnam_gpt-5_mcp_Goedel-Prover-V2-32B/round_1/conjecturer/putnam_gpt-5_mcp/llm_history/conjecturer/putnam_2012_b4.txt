Let
\[
x_n := e^{a_n}.
\]
Then the recurrence
\[
a_{n+1}=a_n+e^{-a_n}
\]
becomes
\[
x_{n+1}=e^{a_{n+1}}=e^{a_n}e^{e^{-a_n}}=x_n e^{1/x_n}.
\]

For \(t\in[0,1]\),
\[
1+t \le e^t \le 1+t+\frac e2 t^2.
\]
Also \(x_{n+1}\ge x_n+1\), so by induction \(x_n\ge e+n\), hence \(1/x_n\le 1\), so the inequality applies with \(t=1/x_n\). Therefore
\[
x_n+1 \le x_{n+1} \le x_n+1+\frac{e}{2x_n}.
\]

Now define
\[
u_n:=x_n-n.
\]
Then \(u_{n+1}\ge u_n\), and
\[
u_{n+1}\le u_n+\frac{e}{2x_n}\le u_n+\frac{e}{2(n+e)}.
\]
Summing gives
\[
u_n=O(\log n).
\]
Hence
\[
x_n=n+O(\log n).
\]

So
\[
a_n-\log n=\log x_n-\log n
=\log\!\left(1+\frac{x_n-n}{n}\right).
\]
But \((x_n-n)/n = O((\log n)/n)\to 0\), so
\[
a_n-\log n \to 0.
\]
Thus the limit exists (with \(L=0\)).
