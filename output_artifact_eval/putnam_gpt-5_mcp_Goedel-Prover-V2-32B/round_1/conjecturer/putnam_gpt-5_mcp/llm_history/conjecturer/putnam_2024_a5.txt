x\cos\theta+y\sin\theta=\rho,
\]
where
\[
\theta=\frac{\alpha+\beta}{2}\pmod\pi,\qquad \rho=9\cos\frac{\alpha-\beta}{2}.
\]
So \(\theta\) is uniform on \([0,\pi)\), and \(\rho\) has density
\[
g(\rho)=\frac1{\pi\sqrt{81-\rho^2}}\qquad(|\rho|<9).
\]

For the unit circle centered at \(C_r=(r,0)\), this line intersects it iff the distance from \(C_r\) to the line is at most \(1\), i.e.
\[
|\rho-r\cos\theta|\le 1.
\]
Thus for fixed \(\theta\), the conditional probability is
\[
F(r\cos\theta),\qquad
F(a)=\int_{a-1}^{a+1} g(\rho)\,d\rho.
\]
Since \(0\le r\le 8\), we always have \([a-1,a+1]\subset[-9,9]\). Also \(g\) is even and strictly increasing on \([0,9)\), so for \(a>0\),
\[
F'(a)=g(a+1)-g(a-1)>0.
\]
Hence \(F(a)\) is strictly increasing in \(|a|\). Therefore for each \(\theta\),
\[
F(r\cos\theta)=F(|r\cos\theta|)
\]
is increasing in \(r\), and strictly so for a positive-measure set of \(\theta\). Averaging over \(\theta\), \(\mathrm{ProbIntersect}(r)\) is strictly increasing on \([0,8]\).
