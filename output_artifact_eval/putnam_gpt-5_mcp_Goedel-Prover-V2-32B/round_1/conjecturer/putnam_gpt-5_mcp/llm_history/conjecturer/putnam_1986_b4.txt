Let  
\[
S=\left\{\sqrt{m^2+2n^2}:m,n\in \mathbb Z\right\}.
\]
The hypotheses on \(G\) say exactly that \(G(r)=\operatorname{dist}(r,S)\), the distance from \(r\) to this set.

We show \(G(r)\to 0\) as \(r\to+\infty\).

For \(r>1\), choose
\[
m=\lfloor r\rfloor,\qquad u=r^2-m^2,
\qquad n=\left\lfloor \sqrt{u/2}\right\rfloor.
\]
Then \(m\in\mathbb Z,\ n\in\mathbb Z\), and since \(m\le r<m+1\),
\[
0\le u=(r-m)(r+m)<1\cdot 2r=2r.
\]
Also, from \(n^2\le u/2<(n+1)^2\),
\[
0\le u-2n^2<2\big((n+1)^2-n^2\big)=4n+2.
\]
Since \(n^2\le u/2<r\), we have \(n<\sqrt r\), so
\[
0\le u-2n^2<4\sqrt r+2.
\]

Now set
\[
s=\sqrt{m^2+2n^2}\in S.
\]
Because \(m^2+2n^2\le m^2+u=r^2\), we have \(s\le r\), hence
\[
0\le r-s=\frac{r^2-s^2}{r+s}
=\frac{u-2n^2}{r+s}
\le \frac{u-2n^2}{r}
<\frac{4\sqrt r+2}{r}\to 0.
\]
Therefore
\[
0\le G(r)\le |r-s|=r-s\to 0,
\]
so indeed \(G\to 0\) at \(+\infty\).

Thus the statement \( \text{Tendsto } G\ \text{atTop}\ (\mathcal N 0)\) is always true for every such \(G\).
