\[
A=(a,1/a),\quad B=(-b,1/b),\quad C=(-c,-1/c),\quad D=(d,-1/d),

with \(a,b,c,d>0\).

Any convex \(S\) containing such points contains their convex hull, hence its area is at least the area of the simple polygon \(ABCD\) (ordered by quadrants I, II, III, IV).

Using the shoelace formula,
\[
[ABCD]
=\frac12\Big(\frac ab+\frac ba+\frac bc+\frac cb+\frac cd+\frac dc+\frac da+\frac ad\Big).
\]
Now for each pair,
\[
\frac ab+\frac ba \ge 2,\quad
\frac bc+\frac cb \ge 2,\quad
\frac cd+\frac dc \ge 2,\quad
\frac da+\frac ad \ge 2
\]
by AM-GM. Therefore
\[
[ABCD]\ge \frac12(2+2+2+2)=4.
\]
So every such convex set has area at least \(4\).

This bound is attained by the square with vertices
\[
(1,1),\ (-1,1),\ (-1,-1),\ (1,-1),
\]
which is convex, has area \(4\), and its four vertices lie on the four required hyperbola branches.

Hence the least possible volume is \(4\).
