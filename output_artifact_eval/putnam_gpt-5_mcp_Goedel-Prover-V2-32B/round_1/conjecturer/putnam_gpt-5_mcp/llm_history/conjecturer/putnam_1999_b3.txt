\[
S(x,y)=\sum_{\substack{m,n\ge 1\\ \frac12\le m/n\le 2}} x^m y^n.

We want
\[

(1-xy^2)(1-x^2y)S(x,y).
\]

Set
\[
H(x,y):=(1-xy^2)(1-x^2y)S(x,y).
\]

For the coefficient of \(x^p y^q\) in \(H\), we get
\[
c_{p,q}
=1_R(p,q)-1_R(p-1,q-2)-1_R(p-2,q-1)+1_R(p-3,q-3),
\]
where \(R(a,b)\) means \(a,b\ge1\) and \(\frac12\le a/b\le 2\).

A Python coefficient check gives the only nonzero coefficients:
\[
(1,1),(2,1),(1,2),(2,2)\text{ with coefficient }1,\quad (3,3)\text{ with coefficient }-1.
\]

So
\[
H(x,y)=xy+x^2y+xy^2+x^2y^2-x^3y^3.
\]

Hence
\[
\lim_{(x,y)\to(1,1)} H(x,y)
=1+1+1+1-1=3.
\]

Therefore the required real number is \(3\).
