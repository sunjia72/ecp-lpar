Let  
\[
P_n=\prod_{k=0}^{n-1}\left(1-\frac1{a_k}\right).
\]

The recurrence is
\[
a_{k}=a_{k-1}^2-2,\qquad a_0=\frac52.
\]

A standard substitution is \(a_k=t_k+t_k^{-1}\). Since
\[
\frac52=2+\frac12,
\]
we can take \(t_0=2\), and then
\[
a_{k+1}=a_k^2-2=(t_k+t_k^{-1})^2-2=t_k^2+t_k^{-2}.
\]
So by induction,
\[
a_k=2^{2^k}+2^{-2^k}.
\]

Now set \(x=\frac12\). Then
\[
a_k=x^{-2^k}+x^{2^k},
\]
hence
\[
1-\frac1{a_k}
=1-\frac{x^{2^k}}{1+x^{2^{k+1}}}
=\frac{1-x^{2^k}+x^{2^{k+1}}}{1+x^{2^{k+1}}}.
\]
With \(y=x^{2^k}\), this is
\[
\frac{1-y+y^2}{1+y^2}
=\frac{1+y^3}{(1+y)(1+y^2)}.
\]
Therefore
\[
P_n
=\frac{\prod_{k=0}^{n-1}(1+x^{3\cdot 2^k})}
{\left(\prod_{k=0}^{n-1}(1+x^{2^k})\right)\left(\prod_{k=1}^{n}(1+x^{2^k})\right)}.
\]

Use
\[
\prod_{k=0}^{n-1}(1+z^{2^k})=\frac{1-z^{2^n}}{1-z}.
\]
Applying this with \(z=x\) and \(z=x^3\),
\[
P_n
=
\frac{\frac{1-x^{3\cdot 2^n}}{1-x^3}}
{\frac{1-x^{2^n}}{1-x}\cdot \frac{1-x^{2^{n+1}}}{1-x^2}}.
\]
As \(0<x<1\), letting \(n\to\infty\) gives
\[
\lim_{n\to\infty}P_n
=
\frac{(1-x)(1-x^2)}{1-x^3}.
\]
Now \(x=\frac12\), so
\[
\frac{(1-\frac12)(1-\frac14)}{1-\frac18}
=
\frac{\frac12\cdot \frac34}{\frac78}
=
\frac37.
\]

So the limit is \(\boxed{\frac37}\).
