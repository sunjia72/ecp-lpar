\[
P(x)=\sum_{k=0}^n a_k x^k,\qquad a_k\in\{\pm1\},\ n\ge1,
\]
and suppose all roots of \(P\) are real.

\[
P(x)=x^n+c_{n-1}x^{n-1}+\cdots+c_0,\qquad c_i\in\{\pm1\}.
\]
Let the real roots be \(r_1,\dots,r_n\).

From Vieta,
\[
\sum r_i=-c_{n-1},\qquad \sum_{i<j} r_ir_j=c_{n-2}.
\]
Hence
\[
\sum r_i^2=\Big(\sum r_i\Big)^2-2\sum_{i<j}r_ir_j
= c_{n-1}^2-2c_{n-2}=1-2c_{n-2}.
\]
Since \(c_{n-2}=\pm1\) and \(\sum r_i^2\ge0\), we must have \(c_{n-2}=-1\), so
\[
\sum r_i^2=3.
\]

Also \(|r_1\cdots r_n|=|c_0|=1\), so
\[
\prod r_i^2=1.
\]
By AM-GM,
\[
\frac{r_1^2+\cdots+r_n^2}{n}\ge (\prod r_i^2)^{1/n}=1,
\]
thus \(3=\sum r_i^2\ge n\). Therefore \(n\le3\).

So only \(n=1,2,3\) are possible.

- \(n=1\): all four degree-1 \(\pm x\pm1\) work.
