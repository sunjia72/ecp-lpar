Reasoning:

- From \(n\mid 2^n\), every prime factor of \(n\) is \(2\), so \(n=2^k\).
- Then \(n-1=2^k-1\mid 2^{2^k}-1\). Using
  \[
  2^a-1\mid 2^b-1 \iff a\mid b,
  \]
  we get \(k\mid 2^k\), so \(k=2^m\).
- Now \(n=2^{2^m}\). The third condition becomes
  \[
  2^{2^m}-2 \mid 2^{2^{2^m}}-2,
  \]
  i.e.
  \[
  2(2^{2^m-1}-1)\mid 2(2^{2^{2^m}-1}-1),
  \]
  so
  \[
  2^{2^m-1}-1\mid 2^{2^{2^m}-1}-1.
  \]
  Again by the same lemma,
  \[
  2^m-1 \mid 2^{2^m}-1,
  \]
  hence \(m\mid 2^m\), so \(m\) is a power of \(2\): \(m=2^r\).

Thus
\[
n=2^{2^{2^r}}.
\]
