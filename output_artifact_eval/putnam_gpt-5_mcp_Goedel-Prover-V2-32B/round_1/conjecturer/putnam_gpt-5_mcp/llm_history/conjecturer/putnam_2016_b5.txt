The intended characterization is:

\[
S=\{x\mapsto x^c \mid c>0\}.
\]

Why:

- Every power map \(f(x)=x^c\) with \(c>0\) works, since
  \[
  x^2\le y\le x^3 \implies x^{2c}\le y^c\le x^{3c}.
  \]
- For the converse, set \(u=\log x>0\) and define \(g(u)=\log f(e^u)\).  
  Then the condition becomes
  \[
  2u\le v\le 3u \implies 2g(u)\le g(v)\le 3g(u).
  \]
- Write \(u=e^s,\ v=e^t\), and define \(k(s)=\log g(e^s)\). Then
  \[
  t-s\in[\log 2,\log 3]\implies k(t)-k(s)\in[\log 2,\log 3].
  \]
- Let \(p(s)=k(s)-s\). Then
  \[
  p(s+\log 2)\ge p(s),\qquad p(s+\log 3)\le p(s).
  \]
  Since \(\log 2/\log 3\) is irrational, the additive subgroup generated by \(\log 2\) and \(\log 3\) is dense; these two monotonicity conditions force \(p\) to be constant.
- Hence \(k(s)=s+C\), so \(g(u)=cu\) for some \(c>0\), and therefore
  \[
  \log f(x)=c\log x \implies f(x)=x^c.
  \]

Python spot-checks also agree: power maps satisfy the condition, while standard non-power examples fail.
