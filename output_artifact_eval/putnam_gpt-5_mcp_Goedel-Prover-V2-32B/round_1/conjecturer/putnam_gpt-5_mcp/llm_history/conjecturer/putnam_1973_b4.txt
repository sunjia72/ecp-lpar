\[
\phi'(y)=\frac1{f'(\phi(y))}\ge 1.
\]

\[
A=\int_0^c (1-\phi(y))\,dy,\qquad
B=\int_0^c 3y^2(1-\phi(y))\,dy.
\]

Write
\[
1-\phi(y)=(c-y)+u(y),
\]
where
\[
u(y)=1-c-(\phi(y)-y).
\]
Since \(\phi(y)-y\) is increasing, \(u\) is decreasing and nonnegative on \([0,c]\). Let
\[
U=\int_0^c u(y)\,dy.
\]
Then
\[
A=\frac{c^2}{2}+U,\qquad
B=\frac{c^4}{4}+3\int_0^c y^2u(y)\,dy.
\]

For any decreasing \(u\ge 0\) on \([0,c]\),
\[
\int_0^c y^2u(y)\,dy\le \frac{c^2}{3}\int_0^c u(y)\,dy=\frac{c^2U}{3},
\]
so
\[
B\le \frac{c^4}{4}+c^2U \le \left(\frac{c^2}{2}+U\right)^2=A^2.
\]
Thus \((\int_0^1 f)^2\ge \int_0^1 f^3\).
