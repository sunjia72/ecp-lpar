Let  
\[
F_c(t)=\sum_{m\ge 0} x_c(m+1)t^m.
\]
From
\[
x_c(k+2)=\frac{c\,x_c(k+1)-(n-k)x_c(k)}{k+1},
\]
one gets
\[
(1-t^2)F_c'(t)=(c-(n-1)t)F_c(t),\qquad F_c(0)=1.
\]
So
\[
F_c(t)=(1-t)^{(n-1-c)/2}(1+t)^{(n-1+c)/2}.
\]

Now \(x_c(n+1)\) is the coefficient of \(t^n\) in \(F_c(t)\).  
If
\[
c=-(n-1),-(n-3),\dots,n-3,n-1,
\]
then \(F_c(t)\) is actually a polynomial of degree \(n-1\), so that coefficient is \(0\). These are \(n\) distinct roots, and \(x_c(n+1)\) is a degree-\(n\) polynomial in \(c\), so these are all the roots. Hence
\[
S=\{-(n-1),-(n-3),\dots,n-1\},
\]
and therefore
\[
\sup S=n-1.
\]

At \(c=n-1\),
\[
F_{n-1}(t)=(1+t)^{n-1}.
\]
Thus for \(1\le k\le n\),
\[
x_{n-1}(k)= [t^{k-1}](1+t)^{n-1}=\binom{n-1}{k-1}.
\]

So the required function is \(\binom{n-1}{k-1}\) (interpreted in Lean via `Int.toNat` and cast to `ℝ`).
