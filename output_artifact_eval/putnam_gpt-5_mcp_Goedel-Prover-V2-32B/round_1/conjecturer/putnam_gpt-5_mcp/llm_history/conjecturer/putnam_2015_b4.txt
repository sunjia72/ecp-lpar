Since only positive triples contribute, we can treat \(a,b,c\) as positive integers.

For fixed \(b,c\), the triangle inequalities give
\[
|b-c|<a<b+c,
\]
so
\[
a=|b-c|+1,\dots,b+c-1.
\]
Hence
\[
\sum_a 2^a = 2^{b+c}-2^{|b-c|+1}.
\]
Therefore
\[
S=\sum_{b,c\ge 1}\frac{2^{b+c}-2^{|b-c|+1}}{3^b5^c}
=\sum_{b,c\ge1}\left(\frac23\right)^b\left(\frac25\right)^c
-2\sum_{b,c\ge1}\frac{2^{|b-c|}}{3^b5^c}.
\]

The first sum is
\[
\left(\sum_{b\ge1}\left(\frac23\right)^b\right)\left(\sum_{c\ge1}\left(\frac25\right)^c\right)
=2\cdot \frac23=\frac43.
\]

\[
\frac{2^{|b-c|}}{3^b5^c}=\frac{2^k}{3^{c+k}5^c}=\left(\frac1{15}\right)^c\left(\frac23\right)^k.
\]
If \(c=b+k\) with \(k\ge1\),
\[
\frac{2^{|b-c|}}{3^b5^c}=\frac{2^k}{3^b5^{b+k}}=\left(\frac1{15}\right)^b\left(\frac25\right)^k.
\]
So
\[
B=\left(\sum_{n\ge1}\left(\frac1{15}\right)^n\right)\left(\sum_{k\ge0}\left(\frac23\right)^k\right)
