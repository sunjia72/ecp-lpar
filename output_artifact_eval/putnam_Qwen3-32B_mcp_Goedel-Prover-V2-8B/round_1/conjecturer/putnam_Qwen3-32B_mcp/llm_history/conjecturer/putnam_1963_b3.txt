The functional equation resembles identities in trigonometry and hyperbolic functions. A natural idea is to test **linear functions**, $ f(x) = ax $, and **hyperbolic sine functions**, $ f(x) = A \sinh(kx) $.

Let $ f(x) = ax $. Then:

$$
f(x)^2 - f(y)^2 = a^2(x^2 - y^2), \quad f(x+y) f(x - y) = (a(x + y)) \cdot (a(x - y)) = a^2(x^2 - y^2),
$$

Thus, the equation holds. This works for all $ a \in \mathbb{R} $, and $ f(x) = ax $ is smooth.

**Sinh case**:  
Let $ f(x) = A \sinh(kx) $. Then using the identity $ \sinh^2 x - \sinh^2 y = \sinh(x + y)\sinh(x - y) $:

$$
f(x)^2 - f(y)^2 = A^2 (\sinh^2(kx) - \sinh^2(ky)) = A^2 \sinh(k(x + y)) \sinh(k(x - y)) = f(x + y) \cdot f(x - y),
$$

So the equation is satisfied for all $ A, k \in \mathbb{R} $, and again, the function is smooth.
