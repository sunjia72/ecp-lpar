Here `s : Fin n → ℤˣ` is just a length-`n` sign pattern, since the only units in `ℤ` are `±1`.

For a permutation `σ` of `Fin (n+1)`, the condition
\[
0 < (s i : \mathbb Z)\,((\sigma(i+1):\mathbb Z)-(\sigma(i):\mathbb Z))
\]
means exactly:

- `s i = 1`  iff `σ(i+1) > σ(i)`,
- `s i = -1` iff `σ(i+1) < σ(i)`.

So `f n s` counts permutations of `0,1,\dots,n` with prescribed up/down pattern `s`.

A classical extremal result for descent patterns is that this count is maximized precisely by the **alternating** patterns. In sign language, that means the maximizing `s` are exactly the two sequences
\[
s_i=\varepsilon(-1)^i \qquad (\varepsilon=\pm1).
\]
Equivalently, consecutive signs always alternate.

So the solution set is the set of all alternating sign patterns.
