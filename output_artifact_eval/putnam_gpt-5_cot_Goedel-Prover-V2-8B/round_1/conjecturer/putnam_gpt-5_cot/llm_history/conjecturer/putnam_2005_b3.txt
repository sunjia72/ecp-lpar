\[
\forall x>0,\quad f'(a/x)=\frac{x}{f(x)}.
\]
We want all positive differentiable functions on \((0,\infty)\) for which this holds for some \(a>0\).

Set \(y=a/x\), so \(x=a/y\). Then the equation becomes
\[
f'(y)=\frac{a}{y\,f(a/y)} \qquad (y>0).
\]
Replacing \(y\) by \(a/y\) also gives
\[
f'(a/y)=\frac{1}{f(y)}\cdot \frac{y}{1}.
\]
A natural guess is a power function \(f(x)=c x^r\). Then
\[
f'(a/x)=cr(a/x)^{r-1},\qquad \frac{x}{f(x)}=\frac{x^{1-r}}{c}.
\]
Matching powers works automatically, and matching constants gives
\[
cr\,a^{r-1}=\frac1c \quad\Longrightarrow\quad c^2 r a^{r-1}=1.
\]
So for any \(r>0\), choosing \(c>0\) and then
\[
a=(c^2 r)^{1/(1-r)} \quad (r\neq 1),
\]
or for \(r=1\), requiring \(c=1\), works. Rewriting in a cleaner parameterization: if
\[
f(x)=b x^c

with \(b>0,c>0\), then the condition is solvable iff either \(c\neq 1\), or \(c=1\) and \(b=1\).

Thus the solution set is exactly the positive power functions, excluding non-unit multiples of \(x\).
