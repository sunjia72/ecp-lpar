Let \(r_{i,p}\) be the \(p\)-th row of \(M_i\) and \(c_{j,p}\) the \(p\)-th column of \(N_j\). Then
\[
(M_iN_j)_{pp}=r_{i,p}\cdot c_{j,p}.
\]
So the condition says:

- if \(i=j\), then every \(r_{i,p}\cdot c_{i,p}\neq 0\);
- if \(i\neq j\), then for some \(p\), \(r_{i,p}\cdot c_{j,p}=0\).

Now form tensors
\[
u_i=r_{i,1}\otimes \cdots \otimes r_{i,n},\qquad
v_j=c_{j,1}\otimes \cdots \otimes c_{j,n}.
\]
Their natural pairing is
\[
\langle u_i,v_j\rangle=\prod_{p=1}^n (M_iN_j)_{pp}.
\]
Hence \(\langle u_i,v_j\rangle=0\) for \(i\neq j\), and \(\langle u_i,v_i\rangle\neq 0\). Therefore the \(u_i\) are linearly independent, so
\[
k\le \dim((\mathbb R^n)^{\otimes n})=n^n.
\]

\[
(M_fN_g)_{pp}=1 \iff f(p)=g(p),
\]
so all diagonal entries are nonzero iff \(f=g\), and if \(f\neq g\) then some diagonal entry is \(0\). Thus the maximum is exactly \(n^n\).
