The game graph is the grid \(P_3^n\) on vertices \(\{0,1,2\}^n\), starting at \(0^n\), and players move along edges without ever revisiting a vertex.

A clean losing strategy for Alice (so a winning strategy for Bob) is given by a pairing of all **non-initial** states:

- For any state \(s \neq 0^n\), let \(i\) be the **largest** coordinate with \(s_i \neq 0\).
- Then \(s_i\in\{1,2\}\). Define \(s^\*\) by changing only that coordinate:
  - \(1 \leftrightarrow 2\),
  - all other coordinates unchanged.

Then:
- \(s^\* \neq s\),
- \(s\) and \(s^\*\) differ by exactly \(1\) in one coordinate, so they are adjacent,
- this is an involution, so it partitions all nonzero states into disjoint adjacent pairs.

Thus Bob can always reply to Alice’s move to some unvisited state \(s\) by moving to its paired partner \(s^\*\). Since paired vertices are used up together, Bob is never stranded immediately after Alice moves. Therefore Alice can never force a win.

So for every \(n \ge 1\), `AliceHasWinningStrategy n` is false. Hence the required predicate is the constant-false function.
