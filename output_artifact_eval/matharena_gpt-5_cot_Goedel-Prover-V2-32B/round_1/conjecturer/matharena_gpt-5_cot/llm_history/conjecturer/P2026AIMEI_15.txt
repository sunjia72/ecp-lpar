Let \(F(m,n)\) be the number of ways to partition a \(2m\times 2n\) rectangle into the minimum possible number of rectangle-loops, namely \(m\) loops when \(m\le n\).

For the \(10\times 10\) board, we want \(F(5,5)\).

Key facts:

1. A rectangle-loop meets any NW-SE diagonal in at most 2 cells.
   So covering the 10 cells of the main diagonal needs at least \(5\) loops.
   Since the problem uses exactly \(5\) loops, this is a *minimum* partition.

2. In a minimum partition, the loop containing the top-left corner is forced to span a whole side:
   - in a non-square \(2m\times 2n\) (\(m<n\)), it must be a full-width top loop of height \(2i\),
   - in a square, it can be either such a full-width top loop or symmetrically a full-height left loop.

3. Hence for \(m<n\),
\[
F(m,n)=\sum_{i=1}^{m} F(i-1,n-1)\,F(m-i,n).
\]
This is exactly the Catalan recurrence, so
\[
F(m,n)=C_m \qquad (m<n),
\]
where \(C_m\) is the \(m\)-th Catalan number.

4. For squares,
\[
F(m,m)=2\sum_{i=1}^{m} F(i-1,m-1)\,F(m-i,m)-F(m-1,m-1),
\]
subtracting the outer-frame case counted twice.
Using \(F(r,s)=C_r\) for \(r<s\), this simplifies to
\[
F(m,m)=2C_m-1.
\]
