\[
\frac{\binom{m}{n-1}}{\binom{m-1}{n}}
= \frac{mn}{(m-n+1)(m-n)},
\]
the inequality
\[
\binom{m-1}{n}<\binom{m}{n-1}
\]
is equivalent (for the relevant maximal \(m\)) to
\[
(m-n+1)(m-n)<mn.
\]
Expanding:
\[
m^2-(3n-1)m+(n^2-n)<0.
\]
So \(m\) must be less than the larger root
\[
r_n=\frac{3n-1+\sqrt{5n^2-2n+1}}{2}.
\]
Hence
\[
M(n)=\lceil r_n\rceil-1,
\]
so
\[
\frac{M(n)}{n}\to \frac{3+\sqrt5}{2}.
\]
