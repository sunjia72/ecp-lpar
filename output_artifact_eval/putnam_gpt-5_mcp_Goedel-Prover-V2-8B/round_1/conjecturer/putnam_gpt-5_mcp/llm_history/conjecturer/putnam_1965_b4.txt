Let  
\[
u_n(x)=\sum_{i=0}^{\lfloor n/2\rfloor}\binom n{2i}x^i,\qquad
v_n(x)=\sum_{i=0}^{\lfloor (n-1)/2\rfloor}\binom n{2i+1}x^i.
\]
Then
\[
u_{n+1}=u_n+xv_n,\qquad v_{n+1}=u_n+v_n,
\]
so whenever the stated denominators are nonzero,
\[
f_{n+1}(x)=\frac{u_{n+1}(x)}{v_{n+1}(x)}
=\frac{u_n(x)+xv_n(x)}{u_n(x)+v_n(x)}
=\frac{f_n(x)+x}{f_n(x)+1}.
\]
Hence
\[
p(h)(x)=h(x)+x,\qquad q(h)(x)=h(x)+1.
\]

For convergence:

- If \(x=t^2\ge 0\), then
  \[
  (1+t)^n=u_n(x)+tv_n(x),\qquad (1-t)^n=u_n(x)-tv_n(x),
  \]
  so for \(t>0\),
  \[
  f_n(x)=t\frac{(1+t)^n+(1-t)^n}{(1+t)^n-(1-t)^n}\to t=\sqrt x,
  \]
  since \(\left|\frac{1-t}{1+t}\right|<1\).  
  For \(x=0\), \(u_n=1,\ v_n=n\), so \(f_n(0)=1/n\to 0=\sqrt0\).

  \[
  u_n(x)+it\,v_n(x)=(1+it)^n.

  \[
  f_n(x)=t\cot(n\theta)
