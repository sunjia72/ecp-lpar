=== CONJECTURER ROUND ===
Let \(P\in \mathbb R^2\) be arbitrary, and choose any nonzero vector \(u\).

Consider the square centered at \(P\) with vertices
\[
A=P-u-iu,\quad B=P+u-iu,\quad C=P+u+iu,\quad D=P-u+iu,
\]
where \(i(x,y)=(-y,x)\) is rotation by \(90^\circ\).

Also let the midpoints of its sides be
\[
N=P-iu,\quad E=P+u,\quad S=P+iu,\quad W=P-u.
\]

By the hypothesis, the sum of \(f\) on the vertices of **every** square is \(0\). So in particular:

- the big square \(ABCD\):  
  \[
  f(A)+f(B)+f(C)+f(D)=0
  \]
- the midpoint square \(NEWS\):  
  \[
  f(N)+f(E)+f(S)+f(W)=0
  \]
- the four corner squares:
  \[
  \begin{aligned}
  f(A)+f(N)+f(W)+f(P)&=0\\
  f(N)+f(B)+f(P)+f(E)&=0\\
  f(W)+f(P)+f(D)+f(S)&=0\\
  f(P)+f(E)+f(S)+f(C)&=0
  \end{aligned}
  \]

Adding the four corner-square equations gives
\[
f(A)+f(B)+f(C)+f(D)+2(f(N)+f(E)+f(S)+f(W))+4f(P)=0.
\]
Using the big-square sum \(=0\) and midpoint-square sum \(=0\), this reduces to
\[
4f(P)=0,
\]
hence \(f(P)=0\).

Since \(P\) was arbitrary, \(f\equiv 0\). Therefore the statement in the theorem is **true**, so the required proposition is `True`.
