\[
Q(ga,gb)=g\sum_{j=0}^{gab-1}(-1)^{\lfloor j/a\rfloor+\lfloor j/b\rfloor}.
\]
Splitting \(j\) into \(g\) blocks of length \(ab\), one gets
\[
Q(ga,gb)=g\,Q(a,b)\sum_{k=0}^{g-1}(-1)^{k(a+b)}.
\]

So it remains to evaluate \(Q(a,b)\) for coprime \(a,b\):

- If one of \(a,b\) is even, then \(a+b\) is odd. Pair \(i\) with \(ab-1-i\). Since
  \[
  \Big\lfloor \frac{ab-1-i}{a}\Big\rfloor=b-1-\Big\lfloor\frac{i}{a}\Big\rfloor,\qquad
  \Big\lfloor \frac{ab-1-i}{b}\Big\rfloor=a-1-\Big\lfloor\frac{i}{b}\Big\rfloor,
  \]
  the two exponents have opposite parity, so terms cancel. Hence \(Q(a,b)=0\).

- If both \(a,b\) are odd, let \(r=i\bmod a\), \(s=i\bmod b\). Then
  \[
  \Big\lfloor\frac{i}{a}\Big\rfloor+\Big\lfloor\frac{i}{b}\Big\rfloor \equiv r+s \pmod 2,
  \]
  and by CRT, \((r,s)\) runs over all pairs \(0\le r<a,\ 0\le s<b\). Thus
  \[
  Q(a,b)=\sum_{r=0}^{a-1}\sum_{s=0}^{b-1}(-1)^{r+s}

  \]

- otherwise \(Q(n,m)=0\).
