Let  
\[
d(x)=\min\bigl(x-\lfloor x\rfloor,\ \lceil x\rceil-x\bigr),
\]
the distance from \(x\) to the nearest integer.

Define
\[
I_n=\frac1n\int_1^n d\!\left(\frac n x\right)\,dx.
\]

Substitute \(t=n/x\). Then \(x=n/t\), \(dx=-n\,dt/t^2\), so
\[
I_n=\int_1^n \frac{d(t)}{t^2}\,dt.
\]

Hence as \(n\to\infty\),
\[
I_n \to \int_1^\infty \frac{d(t)}{t^2}\,dt,
\]
since \(0\le d(t)\le \tfrac12\) and \(\int_1^\infty \frac{dt}{2t^2}<\infty\).

Now split into unit intervals. For integer \(m\ge1\),
\[
\int_m^{m+1}\frac{d(t)}{t^2}\,dt
=\int_0^{1/2}\frac{u}{(m+u)^2}\,du+\int_{1/2}^1\frac{1-u}{(m+u)^2}\,du
=\log\frac{(m+\tfrac12)^2}{m(m+1)}.
\]
So
\[
\int_1^\infty \frac{d(t)}{t^2}\,dt
=\sum_{m=1}^\infty \log\frac{(2m+1)^2}{4m(m+1)}
=\log\prod_{m=1}^\infty \frac{(2m+1)^2}{4m(m+1)}.
\]

For the finite product,
\[
\prod_{m=1}^N \frac{(2m+1)^2}{4m(m+1)}
=
\frac{2N+1}{N+1}\prod_{m=1}^N \frac{4m^2-1}{4m^2}.
\]
Using the given Wallis product
\[
\prod_{m=1}^\infty \frac{4m^2}{4m^2-1}=\frac{\pi}{2},
\]
we get
\[
\prod_{m=1}^\infty \frac{4m^2-1}{4m^2}=\frac{2}{\pi},
\]
so the product above tends to
\[
2\cdot \frac{2}{\pi}=\frac{4}{\pi}.
\]
Therefore
\[
\lim_{n\to\infty} I_n=\log\frac{4}{\pi}.
\]
