=== CONJECTURER ROUND ===
Let  
\[
f(x,y)=\frac{1+2x^2}{1+x^4+6x^2y^2+y^4}-\frac{1+y^2}{2+x^4+y^4}.
\]
Then
\[
I(R)=\iint_{x^2+y^2<R^2} f(x,y)\,dx\,dy.
\]

Use polar coordinates \(x=r\cos\theta,\ y=r\sin\theta\). Then
\[
I(R)=\int_0^R r\left(\int_0^{2\pi} f(r\cos\theta,r\sin\theta)\,d\theta\right)\,dr.
\]

Now
\[
x^4+6x^2y^2+y^4=r^4(\cos^4\theta+6\cos^2\theta\sin^2\theta+\sin^4\theta)
= r^4\frac{3-\cos4\theta}{2},
\]
and
\[
x^4+y^4=r^4(\cos^4\theta+\sin^4\theta)=r^4\frac{3+\cos4\theta}{4}.
\]
Also the \(\cos 2\theta\) parts in the numerators integrate to \(0\), since the denominators depend only on \(\cos 4\theta\). Using
\[
\int_0^{2\pi}\frac{d\theta}{a+b\cos4\theta}=\frac{2\pi}{\sqrt{a^2-b^2}},
\]
we get
\[
\int_0^{2\pi}\frac{1+2r^2\cos^2\theta}{1+r^4(\cos^4\theta+6\cos^2\theta\sin^2\theta+\sin^4\theta)}\,d\theta
=
\frac{2\pi(1+r^2)}{\sqrt{(1+r^4)(1+2r^4)}},
\]
and
\[
\int_0^{2\pi}\frac{1+r^2\sin^2\theta}{2+r^4(\cos^4\theta+\sin^4\theta)}\,d\theta
=
\frac{\pi\sqrt2\,(2+r^2)}{\sqrt{(r^4+2)(r^4+4)}}.
\]

So with \(t=r^2\),
\[
I(R)=\pi\int_0^{R^2}\left[
\frac{1+t}{\sqrt{(1+t^2)(1+2t^2)}}-
\frac{2+t}{\sqrt2\,\sqrt{(t^2+2)(t^2+4)}}
\right]dt.
\]

Split this into the “constant” and “\(t\)” parts.

For the constant part,
\[
\int_0^\infty \frac{dt}{\sqrt{(1+t^2)(1+2t^2)}}
=
\int_0^\infty \frac{\sqrt2\,dt}{\sqrt{(t^2+2)(t^2+4)}},
\]
by the substitution \(t=\sqrt2/u\). Hence those parts cancel in the limit.

For the \(t\)-part,
\[
\int \frac{t\,dt}{\sqrt{(1+t^2)(1+2t^2)}}
= \frac1{\sqrt2}\ln\!\left(\sqrt{t^2+1}+\sqrt{t^2+\tfrac12}\right),
\]
and
\[
\int \frac{t\,dt}{\sqrt2\,\sqrt{(t^2+2)(t^2+4)}}
= \frac1{\sqrt2}\ln\!\left(\sqrt{t^2+2}+\sqrt{t^2+4}\right).
\]
Taking the difference from \(0\) to \(\infty\), the \(\infty\)-terms cancel, leaving
\[
\frac1{\sqrt2}\ln\!\left(\frac{\sqrt2+2}{1+1/\sqrt2}\right)
=
\frac{\ln 2}{\sqrt2}.
\]

Therefore
\[
\lim_{R\to\infty} I(R)=\pi\cdot \frac{\ln 2}{\sqrt2}.
\]

So the required real number is

<<<Real.pi * Real.log 2 / Real.sqrt 2>>>
