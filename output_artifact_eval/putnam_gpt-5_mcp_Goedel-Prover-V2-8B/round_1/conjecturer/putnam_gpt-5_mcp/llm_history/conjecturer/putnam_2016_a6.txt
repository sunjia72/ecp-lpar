=== CONJECTURER ROUND ===
The optimal constant is
\[
\boxed{\frac56}.
\]

A sharp example is
\[
P(x)=1-x(2x-1)^2=-4x^3+4x^2-x+1,
\]
which has degree \(3\), a root at \(x=1\), satisfies \(\max_{[0,1]}|P|=1\), and
\[
\int_0^1 |P(x)|\,dx=\int_0^1 P(x)\,dx=1-\int_0^1 x(2x-1)^2\,dx=1-\frac16=\frac56.
\]
So the least constant is at least \(5/6\).

Now for the upper bound.

Let \(M=\sup_{x\in[0,1]}|P(x)|\). Split \([0,1]\) into maximal subintervals on which \(P\) has constant sign, using its real roots in \([0,1]\). Since \(P\) is cubic and has at least one root in \([0,1]\), there is at most one “leftmost” sign-interval with only one endpoint a root; every later sign-interval has roots at both endpoints.

So it suffices to know two scaled facts for a sign-constant cubic \(f\) on an interval \(I\):

- if \(f\) has a root at one endpoint of \(I\), then
  \[
  \int_I |f| \le \frac56\,|I|\,\|f\|_\infty;
  \]
- if \(f\) has roots at both endpoints of \(I\), then
  \[
  \int_I |f| \le \frac23\,|I|\,\|f\|_\infty.
  \]

The second is easier: after scaling to \([0,1]\), a nonnegative cubic with roots at \(0,1\) is \(f(x)=A\,x(1-x)(x-s)\) with \(s\notin(0,1)\). Then
\[
\int_0^1 f(x)\,dx=\frac23 f\!\left(\frac12\right)\le \frac23\|f\|_\infty.
\]

For the first, after scaling to \([0,1]\), let \(0\le f\le1\) and \(f(1)=0\). Put \(q=1-f\). Then \(q\ge0,\ q(1)=1\), and \(q\) has a zero in \([0,1)\). Minimizing \(\int_0^1 q\) among such cubics gives the extremal
\[
q(x)=x(2x-1)^2,
\]
hence
\[
\int_0^1 q(x)\,dx\ge \frac16,
\qquad
\int_0^1 f(x)\,dx = 1-\int_0^1 q(x)\,dx \le \frac56.
\]
Thus \(\int_I |f|\le \frac56 |I|\|f\|_\infty\).

Now sum over sign-intervals. If the leftmost one has length \(\ell\), then
\[
\int_0^1 |P(x)|\,dx
\le \frac56\,\ell M+\frac23(1-\ell)M
= \left(\frac23+\frac{\ell}{6}\right)M
\le \frac56 M.
\]
So \(c\le 5/6\). Combined with the sharp example, the least constant is exactly \(5/6\).
