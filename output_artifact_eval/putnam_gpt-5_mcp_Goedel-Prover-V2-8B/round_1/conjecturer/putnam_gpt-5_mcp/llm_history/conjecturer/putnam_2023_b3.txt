def longest_zigzag_dp(perm):
    n=len(perm)
    # O(n^2) dynamic programming for LAS
    up=[1]*n
    down=[1]*n
    for i in range(n):
        for j in range(i):
            if perm[j] < perm[i]:
                up[i]=max(up[i], down[j]+1)

Because the \(x_i\in[0,1]\) are independent continuous random variables, ties occur with probability \(0\), and only the relative order matters. So \(L\) has the same distribution as the longest alternating subsequence length of a uniformly random permutation \(\pi_1,\dots,\pi_n\).

For a sequence of distinct reals, let
\[
\sigma_i=\operatorname{sign}(\pi_{i+1}-\pi_i)\qquad (1\le i\le n-1).
\]
A change \(\sigma_i\ne \sigma_{i+1}\) means that \(\pi_{i+1}\) is a local max or local min.  
A standard greedy observation gives:

- taking \(\pi_1\),
- then every interior local extremum,
- then \(\pi_n\),

produces a longest zig-zag subsequence. Hence for \(n\ge 2\),
\[
L=2+\#\{\,i\in\{1,\dots,n-2\}:\sigma_i\ne \sigma_{i+1}\,\}.
\]

So by linearity of expectation,
\[
\mathbb E[L]

\[
