pts = [(x,y) for x in range(-R,R+1) for y in range(-R,R+1)]

Informal proof:

- A side length `1` is impossible.  
  If two lattice points are distance `1`, they differ by `(±1,0)` or `(0,±1)`. WLOG take `A=(0,0), B=(1,0)`.  
  If `AC, BC` are integers, then by triangle inequality
  \[
  |AC-BC| < AB = 1.
  \]
  Since `AC, BC` are integers, this forces `AC=BC`. So `C` lies on the perpendicular bisector of `AB`, namely `x=\tfrac12`, impossible for a lattice point.

- A side length `2` is also impossible.  
  WLOG `A=(0,0), B=(2,0)`. Let `AC=m`, `BC=n` be integers, and let `C=(x,y)` with integers `x,y`. Then
  \[
  x^2+y^2=m^2,\qquad (x-2)^2+y^2=n^2.
  \]
  Subtracting gives
  \[
  m^2-n^2 = 4x-4,
  \]
  so `m^2-n^2` is divisible by `4`, hence `m,n` have the same parity. But triangle inequality gives
  \[
  |m-n| < 2.
  \]

  \[
  1+y^2 = m^2 \implies m^2-y^2=1 \implies (m-y)(m+y)=1,
  \]
