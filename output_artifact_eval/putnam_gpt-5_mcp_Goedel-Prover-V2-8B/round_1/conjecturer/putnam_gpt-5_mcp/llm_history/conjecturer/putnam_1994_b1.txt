\]
We want exactly 15 squares of natural numbers in \(I_n\).

If the 15 squares are
\[
m^2,(m+1)^2,\dots,(m+14)^2,
\]
then necessarily
\[
(m+14)^2-m^2=28m+196\le 500,
\]
so \(m\le 10\).

Also, since there are exactly 15, neither \((m-1)^2\) nor \((m+15)^2\) can lie in \(I_n\).  
For this to be possible at all, we need
\[
(m+15)^2-(m-1)^2 = 32m+224 > 500,
\]
otherwise 16 consecutive squares could fit inside a length-500 interval. Hence
\[
32m+224>500 \implies m\ge 9.
\]
So only \(m=9\) or \(m=10\) are possible.

  \[
  n\ge 23^2-250=279,\quad n\le 9^2+250=331,
  \]
  and exclusions give
  \[
  n>8^2+250=314,\quad n<24^2-250=326.
