Every affine line in the plane meets at least one of them:

- A nonvertical line \(y=mx+b\) meets \(P_+\) iff \(x^2-mx-b=0\) has a real root, i.e. \(m^2+4b\ge 0\).
- It meets \(P_-\) iff \(x^2+mx+b=0\) has a real root, i.e. \(m^2-4b\ge 0\).

These two inequalities cannot both fail, since that would require
\[
b<-\frac{m^2}{4}\quad\text{and}\quad b>\frac{m^2}{4},
\]
impossible. Vertical lines obviously meet both parabolas.
