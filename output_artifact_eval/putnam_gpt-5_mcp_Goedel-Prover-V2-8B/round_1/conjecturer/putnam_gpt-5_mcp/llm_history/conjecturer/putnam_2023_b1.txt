matching \(\binom{m+n-2}{m-1}\).

Informally: in every reachable position, the empty squares form a monotone staircase path from the upper-right corner to the lower-left corner of the \(m\times n\) board. Initially this path is the right edge followed by the bottom edge. A legal move exactly flips one corner of this staircase inside a \(2\times 2\) block, so it preserves the staircase property. Conversely, any such staircase can be obtained from the initial one by repeatedly flipping corners. Thus reachable configurations are in bijection with monotone lattice paths having \(m-1\) down-steps and \(n-1\) left-steps, whose number is
\[
\binom{m+n-2}{m-1}.
\]

So the Lean expression is

<<<fun m n => Nat.choose (m + n - 2) (m - 1)>>>
