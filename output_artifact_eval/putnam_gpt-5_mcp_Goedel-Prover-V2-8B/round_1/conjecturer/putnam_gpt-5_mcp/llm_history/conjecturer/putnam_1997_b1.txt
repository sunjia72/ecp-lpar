Let  
\[
d(x)=\operatorname{dist\_to\_int}(x).
\]
For \(n>0\), the sum is
\[
F(n)=\sum_{m=1}^{6n-1}\min\!\left(d\!\left(\frac{m}{6n}\right),\, d\!\left(\frac{m}{3n}\right)\right).
\]

Using Python to check small cases, one gets:
\[
F(1)=1,\;F(2)=2,\;F(3)=3,\dots
\]
so the answer strongly suggests \(F(n)=n\).

Now for the math:

For \(x\in[0,1]\), \(d(x)=\min(x,1-x)\).  
Define
\[
g(x)=\min(d(x),d(2x)).
\]
A simple case check gives
\[
g(x)=
\begin{cases}
x, & 0\le x\le \frac13,\\[2mm]
1-2x, & \frac13\le x\le \frac12,\\[2mm]
2x-1, & \frac12\le x\le \frac23,\\[2mm]
1-x, & \frac23\le x\le 1.
\end{cases}
\]
Now put \(x=\frac{m}{6n}\). Then:
- for \(1\le m\le 2n\), \(g(x)=\frac{m}{6n}\),
- for \(2n+1\le m\le 3n-1\), \(g(x)=\frac{3n-m}{3n}\),
- and the values from \(3n+1\) to \(6n-1\) are symmetric, while \(m=3n\) contributes \(0\).

So
\[
F(n)=2\left(\sum_{m=1}^{2n}\frac{m}{6n}+\sum_{m=2n+1}^{3n-1}\frac{3n-m}{3n}\right).
\]
Compute:
\[
\sum_{m=1}^{2n}\frac{m}{6n}=\frac{2n+1}{6},
\qquad
\sum_{m=2n+1}^{3n-1}\frac{3n-m}{3n}
=\sum_{k=1}^{n-1}\frac{k}{3n}
=\frac{n-1}{6}.
\]
Hence
\[
F(n)=2\left(\frac{2n+1}{6}+\frac{n-1}{6}\right)=2\cdot \frac{3n}{6}=n.
\]

Thus the solution function is \(n\mapsto n\), i.e. as a Lean expression:

<<<fun n => (n : ℝ)>>>
