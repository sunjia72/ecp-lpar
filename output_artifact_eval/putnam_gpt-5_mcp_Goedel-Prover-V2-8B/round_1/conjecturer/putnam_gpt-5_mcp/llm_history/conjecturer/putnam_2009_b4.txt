\[
H_d(r\cos t,r\sin t)=r^d H_d(\cos t,\sin t),
\]
so
\[
\frac1{2\pi r}\int_0^{2\pi} P(r\cos t,r\sin t)\,dt
\]
is a linear combination of distinct powers of \(r\). Hence \(P\) is balanced iff each homogeneous part has zero circle-average.

- If \(d\) is odd, then \(H_d(-x,-y)=-H_d(x,y)\), so opposite points on the circle cancel, and the average is automatically \(0\).
