## Reasoning

### 1. Condition from \(X\)
Since \(X,B,C\) are collinear and \(AX=AB\), triangle \(ABX\) is isosceles with equal sides \(AB,AX\).

Because \(\beta>\pi/2\), the angle between \(BA\) and the line \(BC\) as used in triangle \(ABX\) is
\[
\angle ABX=\pi-\beta.
\]
Hence
\[
\angle XAB=\pi-2(\pi-\beta)=2\beta-\pi.
\]
But the hypothesis gives
\[
\angle XAB=\frac{\pi-\alpha}{2}.
\]
So
\[
2\beta-\pi=\frac{\pi-\alpha}{2}
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
4\beta=3\pi-\alpha.
\tag{1}
\]

### 2. Condition from \(Y\)
Set \(AB=1\), and use coordinates
\[
A=(0,0),\qquad B=(1,0).
\]
Since \(\angle CAB=\alpha\), the line \(AC\) is the ray
\[
(t\cos\alpha,\ t\sin\alpha).
\]
Point \(Y\) lies on this line and satisfies \(BY=AB=1\). So if
\[
Y=(t\cos\alpha,\ t\sin\alpha),
\]
then
\[
(t\cos\alpha-1)^2+(t\sin\alpha)^2=1
\]
which simplifies to
\[
t^2-2t\cos\alpha=0.
\]
The nonzero solution is \(t=2\cos\alpha\), so
\[
Y=(2\cos^2\alpha,\ 2\sin\alpha\cos\alpha).
\]
Thus
\[
\overrightarrow{BY}
=(2\cos^2\alpha-1,\ 2\sin\alpha\cos\alpha)
=(\cos 2\alpha,\ \sin 2\alpha),
\]
so ray \(BY\) makes angle \(2\alpha\) with the positive \(x\)-axis.

Ray \(BC\) makes angle \(\pi-\beta\) with the positive \(x\)-axis, hence
\[
\angle YBC=(\pi-\beta)-2\alpha.
\]
Given \(\angle YBC=\dfrac{\pi-\beta}{2}\), we get
\[
(\pi-\beta)-2\alpha=\frac{\pi-\beta}{2}
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\beta=\pi-4\alpha.
\tag{2}
\]

### 3. Solve
From (1) and (2),
\[
\frac{3\pi-\alpha}{4}=\pi-4\alpha.
\]
So
\[
3\pi-\alpha=4\pi-16\alpha
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
15\alpha=\pi
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\alpha=\frac{\pi}{15}.
\]

This also gives
\[
\beta=\pi-4\alpha=\frac{11\pi}{15},\qquad
\gamma=\pi-\alpha-\beta=\frac{\pi}{5},
\]
which indeed satisfy
\[
\alpha<\gamma<\frac\pi2<\beta.
\]

Therefore the required constant is
