Let \(S=1+2+\cdots+n=\frac{n(n+1)}2\).

If the numbers \(1,\dots,n\) are split into \(k\) boxes with all box-sums equal, then each box has sum \(S/k\).  
Since the box containing \(n\) must have sum at least \(n\), we need
\[
\frac{S}{k}\ge n.
\]
So
\[
k \le \frac{S}{n}=\frac{n+1}{2},
\]
hence \(k \le \left\lfloor \frac{n+1}{2}\right\rfloor\).

Now this bound is attainable:

- If \(n=2m\) is even, take the \(m\) pairs
  \[
  \{1,2m\},\{2,2m-1\},\dots,\{m,m+1\},

- If \(n=2m-1\) is odd, take
  \[
  \{2m-1\},\{1,2m-2\},\{2,2m-3\},\dots,\{m-1,m\},
  \]
