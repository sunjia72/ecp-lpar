Let \(S_n\) be the set of all \(n\times n\) matrices whose entries are digits \(0,\dots,9\), except the \((0,0)\)-entry is in \(1,\dots,9\).

The key fact is that determinant is multilinear in the columns, so
\[
\sum_{A\in S_n}\det(A)
\]
equals the determinant of the columnwise sums.

- For every column except the first, the sum of all possible columns is a constant multiple of the all-ones vector.
- Hence for \(n\ge 3\), at least two summed columns are equal, so the determinant is \(0\).

- \(n=1\): sum of determinants is \(1+2+\cdots+9=45\).

  \sum ad = 45\cdot 45\cdot 10\cdot 10,\qquad

  \]

  45^2(100-90)=20250.
