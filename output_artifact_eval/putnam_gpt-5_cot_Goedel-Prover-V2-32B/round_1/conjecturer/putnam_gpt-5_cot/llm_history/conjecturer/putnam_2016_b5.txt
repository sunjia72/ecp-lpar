\[
f(x)=x^c \quad (c>0),
\]
since from \(x^2 \le y \le x^3\) we get
\[
x^{2c} \le y^c \le x^{3c},
\]
i.e.
\[
f(x)^2 \le f(y) \le f(x)^3.
\]

\[
g(t)=\log\log(f(e^{e^t})).
\]

if \(\log 2 \le u-t \le \log 3\), then \(\log 2 \le g(u)-g(t) \le \log 3\).
This forces \(g\) to be a translate \(g(t)=t+b\), hence
\[
\log f(x)=e^b \log x,

so \(f(x)=x^c\) with \(c=e^b>0\).
