For base \(b\ge 2\), the recursion is
\[
f_b(1)=1,\quad f_b(2)=2,\quad f_b(n)=n\,f_b(\ell_b(n))\ \ (n\ge 3),
\]
where \(\ell_b(n) = (\text{digits of }n\text{ in base }b).length\).

Iterating,
\[
f_b(n)=n\cdot \ell_b(n)\cdot \ell_b(\ell_b(n))\cdots
\]
until reaching \(1\) or \(2\). So the summand is roughly
\[
\frac1{f_b(n)} \approx \frac1{n \cdot (\text{iterated logs})}.
\]
The convergence/divergence is controlled by how many logarithmic factors appear.

- If \(b=2\), then \(\ell_2(n)\sim \log_2 n\), and iteration gives factors \(n\log n\log\log n\cdots\), enough for convergence.
- If \(b\ge 3\), then \(\ell_b(n)\le \log_2 n\) still, but more importantly one can compare on blocks \(n\in [b^{k-1}, b^k)\), where \(\ell_b(n)=k\). Then
  \[
  \sum_{n=b^{k-1}}^{b^k-1} \frac1{f_b(n)} = \frac1{f_b(k)}\sum_{n=b^{k-1}}^{b^k-1}\frac1n \asymp \frac1{f_b(k)}.
  \]
