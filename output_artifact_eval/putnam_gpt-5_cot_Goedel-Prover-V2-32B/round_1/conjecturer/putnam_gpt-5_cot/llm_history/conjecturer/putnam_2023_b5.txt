Let \(\sigma_m\) be the permutation of the \(n\) residue classes mod \(n\) given by multiplication by \(m\).  
The Lean statement is asking for those \(n\) such that **for every** \(m\) with \(\gcd(m,n)=1\), the permutation \(\sigma_m\) is a square in \(S_n\).

Key facts:

1. A permutation is a square iff for every even cycle length, the number of cycles of that length is even.
2. If \(n=2s\) with \(s\) odd, then divisors come in pairs \(d,2d\), and the cycle contributions from order-\(d\) and order-\(2d\) elements match, so every even cycle count is doubled. Hence every \(\sigma_m\) is a square.
3. \(n=1\) is trivially valid.
4. If \(4\mid n\), take \(m=-1\). Then \(\sigma_{-1}\) has \(2\) fixed points and \((n-2)/2\) transpositions, and this number is odd, so it is not a square.
5. If \(n>1\) is odd, there exists a unit \(m\) with multiplication-by-\(m\) permutation odd (by the Jacobi-symbol/Zolotarev sign criterion), and a square permutation must be even. So no odd \(n>1\) works.
