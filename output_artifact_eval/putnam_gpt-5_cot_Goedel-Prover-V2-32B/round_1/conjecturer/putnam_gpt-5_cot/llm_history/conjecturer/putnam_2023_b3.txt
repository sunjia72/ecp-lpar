For a random sequence of \(n\) independent continuous values in \([0,1]\), ties occur with probability \(0\), so only the relative order matters; this is the same as a uniformly random permutation.

A standard greedy fact is that the longest zigzag subsequence has length equal to the number of local extrema, counting both endpoints. Thus for \(n\ge 2\),

\[
L_n = 2 + \sum_{i=1}^{n-2} I_i
\]

where \(I_i\) indicates that the interior position is a local max or local min. For any fixed interior position, among the three adjacent values, the middle one is either the largest or the smallest with probability \(2/3\). Hence

\[
\mathbb E[L_n]
= 2 + (n-2)\cdot \frac23
= \frac{2(n+1)}{3}.
\]

Since the theorem only constrains the function for \(n\ge 2\), we can use this closed form for all \(n\).
